Isaac Newton

Isaac Newton was born December 25, 1642 January 4, lived in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England, he died March 20, 1727 in London. he was an English physicist and mathematician who was the culminating figure of the Scientific Revolution of the 17th century. In optics, his discovery of the composition of white light integrated the phenomena of colours into the science of light and laid the foundation for modern physical optics. In mechanics, his three laws of motion, the basic principles of modern physics, resulted in the formulation of the law of universal gravitation. In mathematics, he was the original discoverer of the infinitesimal calculus.
